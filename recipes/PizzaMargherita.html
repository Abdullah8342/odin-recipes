Pizza Margherita

A classic Italian pizza, topped with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and basil, representing the colors of the Italian flag. Originating in Naples, it's known for its simplicity and delicious flavor, with a soft, chewy crust.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 ½ cups canned tomatoes (crushed)
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes foamy. Mix flour and salt in a bowl, then add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead until smooth. Let the dough rise for 1-2 hours.
  2. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C).
  3. Roll the Dough: Divide the dough into portions, roll each out into a circle on a floured surface.
  4. Prepare the Sauce: Spread the crushed tomatoes over the dough. Add a pinch of salt.
  5. Add Cheese and Toppings: Place sliced mozzarella evenly over the pizza. Add fresh basil leaves and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Bake the Pizza: Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.
  7. Serve: Let the pizza cool slightly, then slice and enjoy!
