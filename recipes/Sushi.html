Sushi

A Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice, typically accompanied by fresh raw fish or seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. It is often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 Â½ cups water
  • 3 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Fresh fish (e.g., salmon, tuna) or cooked seafood (e.g., shrimp)
  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Vegetables (e.g., cucumber, avocado)
  • Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger (for serving)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the sushi rice until the water runs clear. Cook the rice with water in a rice cooker or on the stovetop. Once cooked, let it cool slightly.
  2. Season the Rice: Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved. Gently fold this mixture into the rice to season it.
  3. Prepare the Fillings: Slice the fish and vegetables into thin strips.
  4. Assemble the Sushi: Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo mat. Spread a thin layer of rice on the nori, leaving a small margin at the top. Add fish and vegetables in the center.
  5. Roll the Sushi: Use the bamboo mat to roll the sushi tightly, then slice the roll into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Serve: Arrange sushi on a plate with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side.
